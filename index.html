Australian Constitutional Values Survey 2025

Understanding Trust in Government and Public Institutions

The Australian Constitutional Values Survey (ACVS) 2025 is a national study examining public trust in government and democratic institutions across Australia.

Conducted by Griffith University in collaboration with Bond University, La Trobe University, and the University of Sydney, this research aims to provide insights into how Australians perceive public officials, government agencies, and democracy.

What is This Survey About?

  • ✔️ Trust in government – Who do Australians trust, and why?
  • ✔️ Public accountability – How does government performance impact trust?
  • ✔️ Democratic values – What shapes attitudes towards governance and reform?

Your participation helps inform policy improvements that enhance trust and democratic engagement across Australia.

Who Can Participate?

  • 🔹 Australian citizens or permanent residents aged 18 years or older
  • 🔹 Selected through a nationally representative sample
  • 🔹 A 15-minute online survey, fully anonymous and confidential

Why is Your Participation Important?

  • 🔹 Completely voluntary – You can withdraw at any time
  • 🔹 Strict confidentiality – No personal details are linked to responses
  • 🔹 Independent research – Conducted in line with the National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research
